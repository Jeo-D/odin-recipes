Doro Wat

What's Doro wat

Doro wat is a spicy Ethiopian chicken stew, often considered the national dish of Ethiopia. It's characterized by its slow-cooked, rich, and flavorful sauce, which includes a generous amount of onions, berbere spice, and often hard-boiled eggs.

Doro wat is not just a dish; it's a culturally significant part of Ethiopian cuisine and is often prepared for special occasions and holidays. There's even a tradition of a man ensuring his wife can make it perfectly before marriage

Steps

The process of making Doro wat listed as follows:
  1. Rub chicken with lemon and salt; marinate if possible.
  2. Sauté onions in niter kibbeh for 5 minutes.
  3. Add tej (if using) and simmer 1 minute.
  4. Stir in most of the berbere; cook 2 minutes.
  5. Add water, boil, then simmer 5 minutes.
  6. Add chicken, boil, then simmer uncovered 45 minutes.
  7. Add fork-pricked eggs; cook 2 minutes.
  8. Sprinkle remaining berbere, stir, and remove from heat.
